About

The Lab

Dielabs investigates how architectures shape workload behavior in AI systems. Focused on the layers that make inference a service: model sharding, runtime configuration, KV cache lifecycle — and the system behavior they produce, from latency and throughput dynamics to performance under load.

I design, benchmark, and validate inference architectures to understand performance, trade-offs, and real-world limits — and turn these findings into practical engineering frameworks for day 0–1 operations, including capacity planning and workload deployment.

Built on 15+ years of enterprise infrastructure experience (VMware, vCloud, Nutanix, NetApp), now fully focused on GPU-accelerated inference stacks and the operational discipline required to run them at scale.
